Clepper diligently recorded all intercepted radio communications over the last two weeks. With the help of his intern, they have analyzed their content to identify important events and relationships between key players. The result is a knowledge graph describing the last two weeks on Oceanus. 1 –  Clepper found that messages frequently came in at around the same time each day.

  1. Develop a graph-based visual analytics approach to identify any daily temporal patterns in communications

To identify daily temporal patterns in communication, we developed an interactive visualization based on the knowledge graph (Figure 1). The x-axis shows hours of the day, while stacked horizontal bands represent 14 consecutive days. Each box marks a communication, color-coded by sender and receiver entity types. A density curve in the background illustrates hourly message volume. Clicking a band expands it to show detailed sender/receiver labels, with options to preview or pin messages for analysis.

Figure 1. Interactive communication graph visualization to aid discovering temporal and semantic patterns.

The left panel includes an entity legend for dynamic filtering, letting users view only messages involving selected entities. Additional filters support topic and keyword-based refinement. When a topic is selected (Figure 2), the opacity of each message and the density curve adjusts according to topic relevance. Multiple topic modeling algorithms are supported, enabling users to explore thematic shifts over time and focus on specific communication patterns or events.

Figure 2. Communications visualization showing the density of messages referring to the topic “reef”, “reef guardian”.

  1. How do these patterns shift over the two weeks of observations?

As shown in Figure 3, we can observe clear temporal shifts in communication patterns across the two-week period. Two distinct day types emerge from the visualization:

  • Planning Days (e.g., Days 1, 5, and 11): These days are characterized by a high concentration of messages in the early morning hour, likely laying the groundwork for upcoming operations or events.
  • Reaction Days (e.g., Days 2, 8, 13, and 14): On these days, we see a marked shift toward increased communication later in the day. This reactive behavior is visually represented by the shift in density and communication volume to the right side of the bands.

In addition to the temporal shifts in communication, Figure 4 reveals corresponding changes in non-communication activity patterns, particularly after the October 7 reports of suspicious activity at Nemo Reef. Starting on October 8, a clear temporal shift emerges in VesselMovement, Monitoring, and Assessment activities to late-night and early-morning windows (e.g., between 2 AM and 5 AM).

3 – It was noted by Clepper's intern that some people and vessels are using pseudonyms to communicate.

  1. Expanding upon your prior visual analytics, determine who is using pseudonyms to communicate, and what these pseudonyms are.
  • Some that Clepper has already identified include: “Boss” and “The Lookout”, but there appear to be many more.
  • To complicate the matter, pseudonyms may be used by multiple people or vessels.

To uncover additional pseudonyms, we worked in the Network Exploration module. From this workspace, the analyst can jump to the Chat View (Figure 14) for message-level detail and to the Entity Similarity Heat-map (Figure 15), which highlights pairs of entities that converse with the same set of partners. Similarity is computed row-by-row: For every entity, we build a vector of its message counts to all other entities, then calculate cosine similarity between those vectors. Thus, if Entity A and Entity B frequently contact the same people, their cells in the heat-map brightly.   1. Describe how your visualizations make it easier for Clepper to identify common entities in the knowledge graph.

Our interface lets the user spot shared identities in a single, fluid workflow. They begin by clicking Person in the legend, which instantly hides vessels, locations, and the other kinds of nodes across the communication graph, relationship graph (Figure 10), and the entity-similarity heat-map (Figure 16), keeping only the Person entities in view. In the heat-map, each bright square marks a pair of people who communicate with almost the same partners; one click on such a square automatically filters both graphs to that pair and opens the Chat View with every message between them, sorted by time (Figure 14). The user can then confirm or reject an alias hypothesis at a glance: if the two IDs scarcely talk to each other in the communication graph, share no formal edge in the relationship graph, and their chat logs contain unusual, repeated details—like Sam and “The Intern” both mentioning new binoculars for the same weekend bird-watching trip—the evidence converges.

c. How does your understanding of activities change given your understanding of pseudonyms?

Unmasking the pseudonyms transformed my reading of the entire radio record. At first I took the chatter at face value: when Sam talked—under his real name—about “going bird-watching,” it sounded like a harmless outing. Once I linked Sam to his alias “The Intern,” it became clear that “bird-watching” is internal code for staking out shipping lanes and logging vessel movements around Oceanus. The same shift happened elsewhere: phrases such as “switch to the secondary channel,” which once seemed like routine radio protocol, are now recognizable as instructions to continue the conversation on a different medium while using aliases only, in order to evade monitoring. In short, knowing who hides behind each pseudonym reveals that many activities presented as leisure, tourism, or everyday logistics are covert surveillance and coordination, key pieces in the corruption network we are trying to expose.

4 – Clepper suspects that Nadia Conti, who was formerly entangled in an illegal fishing scheme, may have continued illicit activity within Oceanus.

  1. Through visual analytics, provide evidence that Nadia is, or is not, doing something illegal.

We created a diagram showing all of Nadia Conti's communications. The x-axis indicates the dates of the messages, and the y-axis indicates the people with whom she communicated. Each point represents a direct (circles) or indirect (orange circles) communication, and the stars indicate the interlocutors who used one or more pseudonyms. Hovering over a point displays a tooltip with the detected keyword and the corruption event that belongs to that message.

We found 10 corruption events and the keywords related to them:

2. permission management: permit, approval, approved, authorization, cancel, canceled, cancellation.

3. illicit payments and bribery: payment, fee, funding, cost, favor, protocol, arrangement.

4. special access: access, special, secured, corridor, entrance.

5. Use of intermediaries: middleman.

6. Confidentiality abuse: confidentiality, encrypted.

7. Suspicious activity: unusual, suspicious, questioning.

8. Unauthorized access: unauthorized.

9. Favoritism: favor.

10. Money laundering: funding.

To identify which text belongs to a corruption event, we read all the messages and identified keywords for each corruption event.

In addition, we have designed a specific diagram to visualize indirect communications—also known as triangulations—which appears in Figure 19. In this graph, each orange dot represents a message that does not originate directly from Nadia Conti, but has been relayed or referenced by a different interlocutor within 24 hours of its original transmission.

This concept of an indirect message captures situations in which information flows through an intermediary before reaching the final recipient, thus revealing less obvious communication paths and allowing us to identify who acts as "bridges" between Nadia and other contacts.

In this way, this diagram complements the analysis of direct communications and helps us better understand the complete dynamics of information exchange surrounding Nadia Conti.

Fig 19. Nadia Conti's indirect communications visualization diagram

We can see that a pseudonym was used in the indirect messages for triangulation, which we consider highly suspicious. In the following graph (Figure 20), indirect communications were highlighted, where it is observed that the majority of indirect messages use a pseudonym for triangulation.

  1. Summarize Nadia’s actions visually. Are Clepper’s suspicions justified?

We implemented an egocentric network graph where Nadia Conti is the central node and the peripheral nodes represent all the entities with which she maintained some type of communication (Figure 21). This visualization uses a circular layout that allows for the analysis of connectivity patterns and relationship types.

The network visually distinguishes between:

- Direct communications (green circles)

- Indirect communications (orange circles)

- Pseudonymous entities (stars)

Clicking on any node in the egocentric network activates a dynamic filter that generates a keyword cloud of corruption events for that entity. This visualization applies text frequency analysis to the keywords in the communications, displaying the most relevant keywords where the size of each term is proportional to its frequency of occurrence.

This feature allows for comparative analysis across different contacts and the identification of recurring themes in the communications of each specific entity.

We also graphed the Timeline of Corruption Events. This interactive multi-line time series graph shows the temporal evolution of different types of corruption events detected in communications. This visualization represents the daily frequency of 10 different categories of corruption indicators over time, where:

- Each colored line represents a specific type of corruption event

- The X-axis shows the temporal progression (dates)

- The Y-axis indicates the number of events per day

- The dots mark the specific days with detected activity

An interactive message viewer has also been added, where we can view messages related to corruption events. These messages can be filtered based on the selected corruption event.
